Gaining Velocity

This week I watched a few videos about vector fields and how they affect different types of simulations. I found it easiest to create normals on points and then rasterize them into a volume (VDB).

I used the cross product to create vectors pointing from the front to the back of the car. To add variation in the velocity volume, I painted masks where I added extra noise. 

Volume slice of the side view

Volume slice of the front view

To mimic the difference in pressure created by a fast moving object,  I created two velocity fields. I used a shrinkwrap and remesh node to obtain lower-poly geometry for simulation purposes. I then used the peak node to displace the low-poly geometry away from the high-poly. I combined different noises and products to create a velocity field that pushes particles in toward the train. I later created another velocity field using the non-peaked points. To mix the values within both fields, I used multiple masks and noises to create a normal vector for each point. I converted the normal attribute to velocity prior to rasterizing the volume.
